### Q1. Which shard failed first and why?

* User-based sharding failed when one user sent many messages
* Channel-based sharding failed fastest due to viral channel traffic
* One shard received most of the load and became overloaded

---

### Q2. Which strategy looked good but failed under spike?

* Hash-based sharding looked balanced under normal load
* Under spike (viral + heavy user), imbalance still occurred
* One shard had higher load (~30%)
* Hashing reduces imbalance but does not eliminate it

---

### Q3. What happens when shards increase (3 → 10)?

* Distribution becomes slightly more balanced
* However, hashing causes data to remap
* Existing data may move to different shards
* This creates consistency and migration problems

---

### Q4. What happens when one shard goes down?

* Messages going to that shard are lost
* System becomes inconsistent
* Some data is missing during queries

---

### Q5. Cross-shard query problem

* To fetch last messages of a channel, all shards must be checked
* Data must be combined and sorted
* This increases query cost and reduces efficiency
